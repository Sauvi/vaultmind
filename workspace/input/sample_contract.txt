ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1.1 "Deliverables" means all software code, documentation, reports, designs,
specifications, and other work product developed by Vendor pursuant to this
Agreement, including all modifications, enhancements, and derivative works.

1.2 "Intellectual Property Rights" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks,
trade secrets, moral rights, and any other intellectual or industrial property
rights, whether registered or unregistered, in any jurisdiction worldwide.

1.3 "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information
disclosed by either Party to the other, whether in oral, written, electronic,
or any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should
be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and
circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to: business plans,
financial data, technical specifications, customer lists, pricing information,
trade secrets, and proprietary technology.

1.4 "Project" means the development of the enterprise resource planning (ERP)
software system as more particularly described in Schedule A attached hereto
and incorporated herein by reference.

1.5 "Milestone" means each phase of the Project as set forth in the Project
Timeline in Schedule B, the completion of which triggers a payment obligation.

1.6 "Source Code" means the human-readable form of the software programs,
including all related documentation, comments, and programmer notes.

1.7 "Acceptance Criteria" means the functional and performance requirements
that the Deliverables must satisfy as specified in Schedule C.

1.8 "Change Order" means a written amendment to this Agreement executed by
authorized representatives of both Parties that modifies the scope, timeline,
or compensation terms.

1.9 "Business Day" means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public
holiday in India.

1.10 "Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond a Party's reasonable control,
including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism,
riots, embargoes, acts of civil or military authorities, fire, floods,
pandemics, epidemics, or failures of third-party telecommunications networks.

ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF SERVICES
================================================================================

2.1 Services. Vendor shall perform the software development and related
services ("Services") as described in Schedule A (Statement of Work) attached
hereto. Vendor shall provide all personnel, equipment, software tools, and
resources necessary to perform the Services, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.2 Project Manager. Vendor shall designate a qualified project manager
("Vendor PM") who shall serve as the primary point of contact for Client.
The Vendor PM shall be responsible for coordinating all aspects of the Project,
managing Vendor's personnel, and providing regular status updates to Client.
Vendor shall not replace the Vendor PM without providing Client with at least
fifteen (15) Business Days' prior written notice and obtaining Client's written
approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

2.3 Key Personnel. The individuals identified in Schedule D as "Key Personnel"
are essential to the performance of the Services. Vendor shall not remove,
replace, or reassign Key Personnel without Client's prior written consent.
In the event that any Key Personnel become unavailable due to resignation,
termination, disability, or other reasons, Vendor shall promptly notify Client
and propose a qualified replacement with equal or greater qualifications,
subject to Client's approval.

2.4 Subcontracting. Vendor shall not subcontract any portion of the Services
to any third party without Client's prior written consent. Any approved
subcontractor shall be bound by terms no less restrictive than those contained
in this Agreement, particularly with respect to confidentiality and intellectual
property. Vendor shall remain primarily liable for all Services performed by
approved subcontractors.

2.5 Change Orders. Either Party may request changes to the scope of Services
by submitting a written Change Order request. Vendor shall provide a written
response within five (5) Business Days detailing the impact of the requested
change on cost, timeline, and resources. No change to the scope, timeline, or
compensation shall be effective unless memorialized in a written Change Order
signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.6 Compliance with Laws. Vendor shall perform all Services in compliance with
all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards, including but not
limited to the Information Technology Act, 2000, the Personal Data Protection
Bill, ISO/IEC 27001 security standards, and any other applicable data
protection and cybersecurity regulations.

ARTICLE 3 — PROJECT TIMELINE AND MILESTONES
================================================================================

3.1 Project Schedule. Vendor shall perform the Services and deliver the
Deliverables in accordance with the Project Timeline set forth in Schedule B.
Time is of the essence with respect to all deadlines and milestone dates
specified in Schedule B.

3.2 Milestone Completion. Upon completion of each Milestone, Vendor shall
deliver to Client written notice of completion along with all required
Deliverables for that Milestone. Client shall have ten (10) Business Days
following receipt of such notice to either: (a) accept the Milestone in
writing, or (b) provide Vendor with detailed written notice of any deficiencies.

3.3 Delay Penalties. In the event that Vendor fails to complete any Milestone
by the scheduled date set forth in Schedule B, and such delay is not caused by
Client or a Force Majeure Event, Vendor shall pay Client liquidated damages in
the amount of Indian Rupees Fifty Thousand (INR 50,000) per Business Day of
delay, up to a maximum aggregate amount of fifteen percent (15%) of the total
Contract Value. The Parties acknowledge that such liquidated damages represent
a reasonable estimate of the losses Client would incur from such delays and
do not constitute a penalty.

3.4 Excusable Delays. Vendor shall not be liable for delays caused directly
by: (a) Client's failure to provide required inputs, approvals, or access
within the timeframes specified in Schedule B; (b) Client-requested Change
Orders that extend the Project Timeline; or (c) Force Majeure Events. Vendor
shall provide written notice of any excusable delay within forty-eight (48)
hours of becoming aware of the delay-causing event.

ARTICLE 4 — COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS
================================================================================

4.1 Contract Value. In consideration for the performance of the Services and
delivery of the Deliverables, Client shall pay Vendor a total fixed fee of
Indian Rupees Forty-Five Lakhs (INR 45,00,000) ("Contract Value"), payable
in accordance with the Milestone Payment Schedule set forth in Schedule B.

4.2 Payment Schedule. Payments shall be made according to the following
Milestone schedule:

    Milestone 1 — Project Kickoff and Requirements Finalization: INR 9,00,000
    (20% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of Agreement execution

    Milestone 2 — System Architecture and Design Approval: INR 6,75,000
    (15% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of Client acceptance

    Milestone 3 — Development Phase 1 Completion (Core Modules): INR 11,25,000
    (25% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of Client acceptance

    Milestone 4 — Development Phase 2 Completion (Integration): INR 9,00,000
    (20% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of Client acceptance

    Milestone 5 — User Acceptance Testing Completion: INR 4,50,000
    (10% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of UAT sign-off

    Milestone 6 — Final Delivery and Go-Live: INR 4,50,000
    (10% of Contract Value) — Due within 7 Business Days of go-live acceptance

4.3 Invoicing. Vendor shall submit invoices to Client's designated billing
contact within five (5) Business Days of achieving each Milestone. All invoices
shall include: Vendor's GST registration number, description of Milestone
achieved, applicable GST amount, and bank account details for NEFT/RTGS transfer.

4.4 Payment Terms. Client shall make undisputed payments within fifteen (15)
Business Days of receiving a valid invoice. Late payments shall accrue interest
at the rate of eighteen percent (18%) per annum, calculated daily from the
due date until the date of actual payment.

4.5 Disputed Invoices. If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client
shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date and provide written notice
of the dispute with supporting documentation within ten (10) Business Days
of receipt of the invoice. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith to
resolve any payment disputes within thirty (30) days.

4.6 Taxes. Each Party shall be responsible for its own income taxes. Vendor
shall be solely responsible for GST compliance and payment on all amounts
invoiced under this Agreement. Client shall deduct TDS as applicable under
the Income Tax Act, 1961, and provide Vendor with Form 16A within the
statutory timeline.

4.7 Expense Reimbursement. Client shall reimburse Vendor for pre-approved,
reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Services,
including travel, accommodation, and equipment costs. All reimbursable expenses
must be pre-approved by Client's Project Manager in writing and supported by
original receipts. Expense claims must be submitted within thirty (30) days
of incurring the expense.

ARTICLE 5 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS
================================================================================

5.1 Work Made for Hire. All Deliverables developed by Vendor under this
Agreement shall be considered "works made for hire" as defined under applicable
copyright law. To the extent any Deliverables do not qualify as works made for
hire, Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to Client all right, title, and interest
in and to such Deliverables, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein,
throughout the world, in perpetuity.

5.2 Pre-existing IP. Vendor retains ownership of all software tools,
frameworks, libraries, methodologies, and know-how developed by Vendor prior
to or independently of this Agreement ("Vendor Background IP"). To the extent
any Vendor Background IP is incorporated into the Deliverables, Vendor hereby
grants Client a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable license
to use, modify, and distribute such Vendor Background IP solely as part of
the Deliverables.

5.3 Source Code Escrow. Vendor shall place all Source Code in escrow with
a mutually agreed escrow agent within thirty (30) days of this Agreement's
execution. Client shall have the right to access the escrowed Source Code
upon: (a) Vendor's insolvency or cessation of business; (b) Vendor's material
breach of this Agreement that remains uncured for more than thirty (30) days;
or (c) termination of this Agreement by Client for cause.

5.4 Open Source Software. Vendor shall not incorporate any open source software
into the Deliverables without Client's prior written approval. Vendor shall
provide Client with a complete inventory of all open source components used,
including applicable license terms, within fifteen (15) days of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY
================================================================================

6.1 Confidentiality Obligations. Each Party agrees to: (a) hold the other
Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same
degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no
less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any
third party without prior written consent; and (c) use Confidential Information
solely for the purposes of performing obligations under this Agreement.

6.2 Permitted Disclosures. Confidential Information may be disclosed to a
Party's employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and are
bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those herein.

6.3 Exceptions. Confidentiality obligations shall not apply to information
that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving
Party; (b) was rightfully known to the receiving Party before disclosure;
(c) is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of
Confidential Information; or (d) must be disclosed pursuant to applicable
law or court order, provided the receiving Party provides prompt written
notice to the disclosing Party and cooperates with any protective order.

6.4 Duration. Confidentiality obligations shall survive expiration or
termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except with
respect to trade secrets, which shall be protected indefinitely.

6.5 Data Protection. Vendor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical
and organizational security measures to protect Client's data, including
encryption of data at rest and in transit, access controls, and regular
security audits. Vendor shall promptly notify Client of any actual or suspected
data breach within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery.

ARTICLE 10 — TERM AND TERMINATION
================================================================================

10.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue
until the completion and final acceptance of all Deliverables, unless earlier
terminated in accordance with this Article ("Term").

10.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon
written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and
fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written
notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; (b) becomes insolvent,
makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is subject to bankruptcy
or insolvency proceedings; or (c) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary
course.

10.3 Termination for Convenience. Client may terminate this Agreement for
convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to Vendor. In such
event, Client shall pay Vendor for all Services satisfactorily performed and
accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus reasonable documented
wind-down costs not to exceed ten percent (10%) of the unpaid Contract Value,
provided Vendor takes all reasonable steps to mitigate such costs.

10.4 Termination for Non-Payment. Vendor may suspend Services upon thirty (30)
days' written notice if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount due and such
failure continues for fifteen (15) days after the due date. If Client fails to
make payment within the thirty-day notice period, Vendor may terminate this
Agreement.

10.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination: (a) each Party shall promptly
return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information; (b) Client shall
pay all amounts due for Services performed and accepted prior to termination;
(c) all licenses granted herein shall terminate, except as provided in
Section 5.2; and (d) Articles 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 11 shall survive.

10.6 Transition Assistance. Upon termination or expiration, Vendor shall
provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days at
Client's written request, at Vendor's then-current standard rates. Such
assistance shall include knowledge transfer, documentation, and reasonable
cooperation with any successor vendor.

ARTICLE 11 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION
================================================================================

11.1 Informal Resolution. In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim
arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt
to resolve such dispute through good-faith negotiations between senior
representatives of each Party for a period of thirty (30) days following
written notice of the dispute ("Negotiation Period").

11.2 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved during the Negotiation Period,
either Party may submit the dispute to mediation administered by the Indian
Mediation Centre or such other mediation body as the Parties may agree. The
costs of mediation shall be shared equally between the Parties.

11.3 Arbitration. If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within sixty (60)
days of commencement, either Party may submit the dispute to binding
arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. The arbitration
shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator mutually agreed upon by the Parties,
or failing agreement, appointed by the High Court of Bombay. The seat and
venue of arbitration shall be Mumbai, Maharashtra. The language of arbitration
shall be English. The arbitral award shall be final and binding.

11.4 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in
accordance with the laws of India, without giving effect to any choice of
law or conflict of law provisions. Subject to the arbitration clause above,
the Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts in Mumbai,
Maharashtra.

11.5 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek
injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction
to prevent irreparable harm, including with respect to breaches of Articles
5 or 6, without first complying with the dispute resolution procedures herein.

12.2 Amendments. No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of

representatives of both Parties.

12.3 Waiver. No waiver by either Party of any breach or default shall
constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach or default. Failure to enforce

such provision in the future.

12.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or
unenforceable, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary
to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue
in full force and effect.

12.5 Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights or
obligations hereunder without the other Party's prior written consent, which
shall not be unreasonably withheld. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either
Party may assign this Agreement without consent in connection with a merger,
acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, provided the
assignee assumes all obligations hereunder.

12.6 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure
in performance resulting from a Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall
promptly notify the other Party of the Force Majeure Event and its expected
duration. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) days,
either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice
without liability, except for payment of amounts already due.

12.7 Independent Contractors. The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing
in this Agreement shall create or be deemed to create a partnership, joint
venture, agency, employment, or fiduciary relationship between the Parties.

12.8 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and
delivered by: (a) hand delivery with written acknowledgment; (b) nationally
recognized overnight courier; or (c) email with confirmation of receipt, to
the addresses specified on the signature page. Notices shall be effective upon

12.9 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of
which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute
one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.

12.10 No Third-Party Beneficiaries. This Agreement is for the sole benefit
of the Parties and their permitted successors and assigns. Nothing herein
shall create any rights in any third party.

SCHEDULE A — STATEMENT OF WORK

The Project scope includes development of an integrated ERP system comprising:

Module 1: Financial Management System
- General Ledger with multi-currency support
- Accounts Payable and Receivable automation
- GST-compliant invoicing and reporting
- Bank reconciliation and treasury management
- Financial reporting and MIS dashboards
- Integration with existing banking partners

Module 2: Human Resource Management
- Employee master data management
- Payroll processing with statutory compliance (PF, ESI, TDS)
- Leave and attendance management
- Performance appraisal workflow
- Training and development tracking

Module 3: Supply Chain Management
- Vendor management and procurement
- Purchase order and approval workflows
- Inventory management with warehouse support
- Logistics tracking and delivery management
- Vendor payment automation

Module 4: Customer Relationship Management
- Lead and opportunity management
- Customer 360-degree view
- Service ticket and escalation management
- Customer communication history
- Sales forecasting and pipeline reporting

Module 5: System Integration and Infrastructure
- REST API development for third-party integrations
- Single Sign-On (SSO) implementation
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Data migration from legacy systems
- Performance testing and security audit

SCHEDULE B — PROJECT TIMELINE

Milestone 1: Requirements Finalization — February 15, 2025
Milestone 2: Architecture Approval — March 15, 2025
Milestone 3: Development Phase 1 — June 30, 2025
Milestone 4: Development Phase 2 — September 30, 2025

SCHEDULE C — ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

System Performance:
- Page load time: < 3 seconds under normal load
- Concurrent users: minimum 500 without degradation
- System availability: 99.5% uptime during business hours
- Data backup: automated daily backups with 30-day retention
- Disaster recovery: RTO of 4 hours, RPO of 1 hour

Security Requirements:
- ISO/IEC 27001 compliance
- OWASP Top 10 vulnerability remediation
- Data encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Multi-factor authentication for all admin users
- Quarterly penetration testing
